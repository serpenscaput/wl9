January 31, 2010

Here we go again...

I have been working on this map replacement set ever since the release of the original acktung! six years ago.  Not that it has taken me that long to build and test the maps, but other projects (like HoS/Orb, etc.) had taken priority at the time.  The original set was designed to be fun and to not take itself too seriously, much how I view the maps in the commercial game.  The Last Waltz is much more polished, but I think it is still a fun play.  Only time will tell.

Why call it The Last Waltz?  I think I can safely say that I will never do another 60-level map replacement set.  Too draining and definitely too time consuming.  I don't even want to estimate the time it took to design and play test these levels.  It was a labour of love though as I am still as passionate for this game as I was back in the early 90's.

I will admit to getting lazy in one area.  I have ignored changing the par times.  My bad.  I don't have a hex editor or Borland installed on this PC and I didn't feel like going through all of the trouble of installing anything.  If this is an issue then you have my humblest of apologies.

I guess the only other issue is why I didn't jump on the SDL bandwagon.  Truth be told, I haven't played a single SDL mod past the first level.  Since I cannot configure the movement keys to my liking (I use the keypad), I can't be bothered to re-train myself to use different keys to play.  I am one old dog that isn't willing to learn any new tricks.
