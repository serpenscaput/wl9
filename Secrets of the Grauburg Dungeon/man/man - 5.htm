Story of Grauburg
Grauburg is a little town somewhere in the Alps. It was founded in 987. In 1023 it was invaded by some tribe from the East, which built a giant underground sanctuary. They made bloody sacrifices for their gods - animal and human sacrifices. According to the legends, the mysterious tribe disappeared after an earthquake, which had partially destroyed their sanctuary. Since then, Grauburg was a peaceful place for hundreds of years and the old sanctuary was forgotten.
In 1935 a young German archeologist, Herman Blausteiner, arrived to Grauburg with his family for their holidays. One day when he was walking in the nearby mountains, he found a small golden skull. After examining it he found out, that it had been created hundreds of years before.
