.... Level 2 - Subterranean mines

     These mines are being used by the nazis to extract rare black diamonds to help fund the rebuilding
     of their "New Reich". Other treasure items are also being kept in storage in this underground den.

     1: BJ starts here.
     2: This secret area should be your first stop - a very handy machine gun can be found here. But don't waste
          time getting here - guards patrol the area and you'll be spotted before you get the machine gun if you
          take too long!
     3: Needless to say, this secret area should be your next stop. The first aid kits here give you a place to run
          to should your health drop too low.
     4: This secret pushwall opens up to some nice stores of treasure, plus there's some health and ammo
          to restore anything you lose dispatching the clones in the area.
     5: Make your way through the mines to the bronze key located in this room.
     6: Use the secret pushwalls to access this Artefact of Life should your battle scars be getting the better
          of you!
      7: Once you have the bronze key, you'll have to backtrack your way through the mines to this area. You'll
          need the blood key here to proceed towards the last part of the level.
     8: The gold key is located behind a breakable column - just make sure you take care of the clones in
          the area first.
     9: Although you can access this room via the breakable column, the best strategy is to get the gold key first,
          then open the locked door with the explosive barrel just behind it. Stand back and shoot the barrel, and
          you should clear this room of most of its enemies, allowing you to pick up the elevator key without a fight!
   10: On to level 3!
 
 































.    Enemies marked in blue appear in all difficulty levels. Enemies marked in green
       appear only in difficulty levels "I'm a Hero" & "Suicidal Tendencies". Enemies
       marked in white appear only in the difficulty level "Suicidal Tendencies".
       Locked doors appear in the colour of the key required to open them, whilst unlocked
       doors are coloured grey (or green if it's an unlocked elevator door).
