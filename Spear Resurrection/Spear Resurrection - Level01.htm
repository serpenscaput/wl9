.... Level 1 - The Black Forest

   This forest has been booby trapped by the nazis with landmines, and is guarded by a number of guards and
   "Suicide Clones". The landmines are hard to see, but if you look carefully, you'll see a grey line marking the
   ground where they've been buried. The best way to survive this level is to use stealth, and to remain alert!
   For those willing to take the time to explore, plenty of rare black diamonds (worth 2500 points each) can be
   found in this level.

  1: BJ starts here.
  2: Need a pistol? The guard just around the corner will be happy to give you his, provided you kill him first
       that is.
       The best way to do that is to let him see you, then hide around the corner as he runs into that landmine
       blocking his path! Voila! The pistol is yours!
       NOTE: Areas after this point are littered with landmines. They're hard to see, and can be planted amongst
       vegetation making it even harder. Watch your step, and hope the enemy don't watch theirs! And watch for
       Suicide Clones - they're silent, but deadly!
   3: Detour to this area to find a secret enclosure built into the hillside. There are some handy ammo clips
       and a few black diamonds from the mines hidden within.
   4: Follow the red dotted line for a quick exit - handy for beating par times, or for just making a quick exit from
       this forest of misery! Or, you may prefer to explore other areas in search of rare black diamonds.
   5: 10,000 points worth of treasure in this secret area - may be worth your while.
   6: Don't shoot the guard here - let him set off a mine which will save you ammo, and clear a path for you to
       access those treasure items he was guarding.
   7: On to level 2!
 
 































.    Enemies marked in blue appear in all difficulty levels. Enemies marked in green
       appear only in difficulty levels "I'm a Hero" & "Suicidal Tendencies". Enemies
       marked in white appear only in the difficulty level "Suicidal Tendencies".
       Locked doors appear in the colour of the key required to open them, whilst unlocked
       doors are coloured grey (or green if it's an unlocked elevator door).
