CONTENTS:

This zip contains two directories (shiggles2 & shiggles3 – yes, I stayed up all night coming up with that name).  Both of those directories contain five files each.  In each directory you will find two SDL files, the EXE, a GAMEMAPS and a MAPHEAD file.  The files in shiggles2 should be placed in your ‘Return to Danger’ folder and I bet you know where shiggles3 goes (and if you said the recycle bin I will give you SUCH a slap).  Yes, shiggles3 goes into your ‘Ultimate Challenge’ folder.  Back up your files before hand if you wish (ok, ok, everybody knows that, but ya gotta know there is one idjit in the crowd – there ALWAYS is!  Somebody – SOMEBODY is going to put these in their SoD folder and then proclaim me the moron for making levels that look stupid – just watch – IT WILL HAPPEN!  lololol).  The extensions for GAMEMAPS and MAPHEAD in both instances have been renamed .SOD from .SD2 and .SD3 respectively.  I cannot be sure from where you got the necessary files (like the VSWAP) to run these games and I cannot assume everybody has the Super CD Pack.  If you have multiple VSWAPS in your SOD directory you might notice they have different extension.  You will have to manually rename each one to coincide with the corresponding GAMEMAP/MAPHEAD combination.  Clear as mud?  This is why I suggest have a Spear, Return to Danger, and an Ultimate Challenge directory. 

DOS vs. SDL (best 2 outta three by pinfall - no count out)

I was remiss in the original README (which you are obviously NOT reading now) by neglecting to mention that these levels should work just fine with the original SPEAR.EXE that came with the original game (like the ever rare triple negative?).  These levels were designed in the DOS environment so I hoping that whatever issues, if any, that happen with the game should be of a very minor nature and purely a result of crappy mapping on my part (I do have a reputation, don't ya know).  When I requested the SDL.EXE from Andy I asked that it be very vanilla in nature as I am very vanilla in nature.  I didn't want any bells or whistles, just an EXE that would work on modern PCs and one where there wouldn't be any issues with push walls not moving the required number of spaces.  What was added, and I am quoting straight from Andy:

The EXE is quite vanilla, and the maps do stay under the object limit. The noticeable coding changes: 
- Tab+S toggles the hitwall/spacebar sounds 
- Saved games and config have unique names allowing DOS version to reside in same folder 

This was so that DOS and SDL fans can be united.  To hell with segregation, I say!!!  Well, there will always be the stigma on us DOS-hounds that the true original diehards must face.  We are an exclusive club - probably less than 10 of us remaining.  We meet in Zurich every two years.  The password is the true acronym of MCS (and please don't say 'multi-cannon shredder' as you might make me cry).  I need a cookie.
